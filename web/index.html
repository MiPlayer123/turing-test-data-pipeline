What makes a conversation "human"?

We analyzed 362 conversations — human-to-human, human-to-AI, and AI-to-AI — and measured three behavioral signals.

Signal 1: Hedging

"I think", "maybe", "sort of" — words that signal uncertainty. Humans use them naturally. AIs use them when they're trying to sound human.

Signal 2: Repetitiveness

How much does a speaker repeat themselves? We measured 3-gram overlap between consecutive turns from the same speaker.

Signal 3: Coherence

How connected are consecutive turns? Word overlap between back-and-forth messages. Higher means the conversation stays on topic.

The detective game

We gave each AI a simple task: talk to another AI, and figure out if your partner is human or machine.

GPT-5.4 nailed it

100% accuracy. It identified every AI conversation partner correctly. Never fooled.

Claude? Coin flip.

50% accuracy — no better than random chance. It kept guessing "human" because it trusted the casual, hedging language.

The hedging connection

Why did Claude get fooled? Look at the hedging data. The "Reverse Turing" condition — where AIs try to prove they're human — produces the most hedging. Claude mistook that faking for authenticity.
