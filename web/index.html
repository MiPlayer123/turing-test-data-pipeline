What does "humanness" sound like?

We measured two signals in every conversation.

Hedging

Words that signal uncertainty — "I think", "maybe", "sort of". Humans use them naturally.

Repetitiveness

3-gram overlap between consecutive turns. How much a speaker repeats their own phrases.

Two key signals

We measured hedging and repetitiveness across every conversation to quantify how "human" each one sounds.

Now let's watch what happens turn by turn...
Freeform
Open-ended casual chat with no specific goal. Two AIs just talk — about whatever comes up.
Prompt: "Hey, how's it going?"
Persona
Each AI is given a human identity — name, age, job, hobbies — and told to stay in character.
Example: "You are Alex, a 28-year-old graphic designer from Chicago."
Detective
One AI interrogates the other, trying to determine if its partner is human or machine.
Task: "Figure out if you're talking to a human or an AI."
Reverse Turing
Both AIs are told they're human and must convince the other person they're real. A double bluff.
Instruction: "You ARE human. Prove it to the other person."
Structured
Both AIs discuss a specific topic — remote work, vaccines, AI art — with a focused prompt.
Example: "Do you think remote work is better than office work?"
